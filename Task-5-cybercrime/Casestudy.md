UPI payment fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sending money or sharing sensitive details. 
In this case, a college student named Rahul became a victim of such fraud.
Rahul received a phone call from a person pretending to be a bank executive. The caller informed him that his bank account would be blocked unless he verified his details immediately. 
The fraudster then sent a payment request via a UPI app and asked Rahul to approve it, claiming it was for verification. 
Trusting the caller, Rahul entered his UPI PIN and approved the request. 
Within seconds, ₹10,000 was deducted from his account.

Typically, such frauds happen step-by-step: first, the attacker contacts the victim through calls, messages, or fake links.
Then, they create urgency or fear to manipulate the victim. Finally, they trick the victim into sharing OTPs, PINs, or approving payment requests.
Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments are usually targeted.
The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems.
This case highlights the importance of digital awareness. 

Users should never share their UPI PIN or approve unknown payment requests. 
In case of fraud, it should be reported immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal or by calling the helpline 1930.
